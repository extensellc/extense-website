The Ultimate DITA Migration Playbook

Don't just copy-paste. Transform. A step-by-step guide to moving from Word/Markdown to DITA XML.

Phase 1: Analysis & Strategy

Before you convert a single file, you must understand your current content estate. "Garbage in, Structured Garbage out."

Step 1: The Content Audit

Catalog everything. How many PDFs? How many duplicates? Identify the "Redundant, Obsolete, Trivial" (ROT) content and delete it. Do not migrate ROT.

Step 2: Information Architecture

Define your Topic Types. Concept, Task, Reference, Troubleshooting. Define your taxonomy and metadata strategy. How will users filter this content?

Step 3: Pilot Migration

Take 50 representative pages. Convert them manually or via script. Test the output. Refine the rules. Do not skip this.

Phase 2: Conversion & Enrichment

Step 4: Batch Conversion

Feed your documents through the conversion engine. Leverage context-based pattern matching — not just style mapping — to differentiate between "numbered steps in a task" and "numbered lists in a reference topic". Our engine converts complex tables (row/column spanning), FrameMaker equations to MathML, and embedded drawings to SVG automatically.

Step 5: Deduplication & Enrichment

After conversion, run automatic exact-topic deduplication. Migrate identifies redundant topics across your entire document collection and consolidates them into reusable warehouse topics. Enrich content by injecting metadata, keys, conref targets, and taxonomy attributes during conversion — not after.

💡 Pro Tips From 2M+ Pages of Migration Experience

  • Delete ROT first. Redundant, Obsolete, Trivial content should never be migrated. We've seen teams migrate 40% dead content.
  • Pilot with 50 pages. Not 5, not 500. Fifty gives statistical significance without overwhelming the team.
  • Don't fix formatting in source. Fix it in DITA stylesheets after conversion. Source cleanup is wasted effort.
  • Convert all languages simultaneously. Don't migrate English first and "do translations later." The TM alignment breaks.
